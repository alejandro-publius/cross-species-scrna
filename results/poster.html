Aligning Human & Mouse Islet Cells in a Shared Latent Space

Mapping cell-type phenotypes from human to mouse and separating conserved from species-specific gene programs — from raw counts to an evaluated, sanity-checked model.

Personal learning project. Results are association, not mechanism. No organizational affiliation implied. Baron et al. 2016 islet data (GSE84133).

Pipeline

1
Data
Baron GSE84133 raw UMI counts, author cell-type labels; QC + log-normalize.
2
Shared gene space
MGI homology → 1:1 orthologs. Case-aware (INS vs Ins1), guarded by an assertion.
3
VAE from scratch
Raw PyTorch, negative-binomial likelihood, explicit ELBO — comprehension insurance.
4
scVI integration
Species as the batch covariate → shared latent that mixes species, keeps cell types.
5
Supervised contrastive
Trained on human labels only (mouse held out) to test cross-species transfer honestly.

Honest limitations

  • Balanced acc (0.86) < raw acc (0.94): rare types (schwann, macrophage) transfer worse.
  • 1:1 ortholog restriction drops duplicated gene families — they are invisible, not "specific".
  • Conserved/specific is descriptive marker overlap, not a causal claim.
  • Baron is small & clean; a real atlas has nested, confounded batch structure.
